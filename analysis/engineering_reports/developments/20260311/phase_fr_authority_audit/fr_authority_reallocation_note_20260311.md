# FR Authority Reallocation Note

Date: 2026-03-11
Thread: `analysis/engineering_reports/developments/20260311/phase_fr_authority_audit`
Status: bounded engineering note for governance review

Question:

```text
Which geometry authorities were historically preempted by FR,
which of those should remain in FR,
and which should be reduced or freed so later mechanisms can express themselves on a cleaner substrate.
```

Working canonical position:

```text
FR = resistance to formation deformation
```

This note does not propose a semantic rewrite.
It reframes the current defect hypothesis from local over-strength to authority preemption and reallocation.

## 1. Historical-Rooted Mechanism Interpretation

The current FR issue should not be read only as:

- FR too strong
- FR too high at the top end
- FR needing a local threshold fix or cap

Those descriptions are operationally useful, but they are not the deepest explanation.

The stronger root-cause reading is:

```text
FR was one of the earliest runtime personality authorities to express directly into geometry.
Later mechanisms such as MB, PD, ODW, and multiple movement revisions
were layered onto a geometry field that FR had already strongly preconditioned.
```

Engineering reading:

- later mechanisms were not necessarily added onto a neutral posture substrate
- instead, they may have been entering a geometry regime already shaped by FR-led persistence and pre-contact restoration
- as a result, later mechanisms may look weaker, more conditional, or more dependent on FR than their intended semantic role would justify

This is why the current defect hypothesis is better stated as:

```text
authority preemption / accumulated coupling problem
```

rather than only:

```text
local over-strength problem
```

The practical implication is important:

- a pure cap or threshold patch may reduce symptoms
- but it may still leave later mechanisms speaking through an FR-preconditioned geometry substrate

So the present goal is not merely to weaken FR.
It is to identify where FR is carrying geometry authority that should not primarily live there.

### 2.1 Legitimate deformation resistance

Current reading:

- FR helping a formation resist distortion
- FR helping regroup toward a fleet center / structural reference
- FR helping hold shape once contact pressure begins

Why this belongs in FR:

- this is directly consistent with canonical meaning
- without some FR expression here, the parameter would lose its basic stabilizer role

Current governance label:

- `should remain in FR`

### 2.2 Tolerated shape persistence influence

Current reading:

- FR helping an already-emerging deformation remain visible for longer
- FR helping front asymmetry survive contact buildup and local turbulence

Why this is only partly legitimate:

- persistence is close to deformation resistance
- but persistence can easily slide into "which shapes survive long enough to count as emergence"

Current governance label:

- `should remain partly in FR`
- but likely `should be reduced in FR` where it becomes early gate authority

### 2.3 Suspected emergence-threshold control

Current reading:

- FR appears to alter whether weak protrusions survive long enough to become observed wedge-like geometry at all
- this is especially visible in the controlled DOE:
  - `FR=2`: `0/18` wedge-capable cells
  - `FR=5`: `5/18`
  - `FR=8`: `16/18`

Why this is suspicious:

- this pattern looks closer to gate authority than pure stabilization
- it makes FR look like a selector of geometric outcome classes, not just a holder of already-selected forms

Current governance label:

- `should be reduced in FR`

### 2.4 Suspected movement-level geometry authority

Current reading:

- the active `v3a` centroid-restoration term is always on across pre-contact, emergence, and carry-through
- this gives FR direct leverage over geometry before later posture or conversion signals have fully expressed themselves

Why this is suspicious:

- it lets FR act too early and too continuously
- that creates a strong chance that the movement substrate itself is already biased before ODW / MB / PD can fully differentiate their roles

Current governance label:

- `should be re-expressed through movement substrate`

Meaning:

- movement substrate should remain capable of expressing posture differences without requiring FR to open the geometry channel first

### 2.5 Suspected posture-bias authority now preempted by FR

Current reading:

- ODW is readable, but mostly once FR has already opened a viable geometry channel
- in the DOE, ODW improves structural quality and readability more clearly than it creates posture from a cold start

Why this suggests preemption:

- if ODW is intended to be a posture-support or posture-direction family, it should not have to wait for FR to first legitimize the geometry state
- current behavior suggests some posture-expression space may already be occupied upstream by FR-led geometry conditioning

Current governance label:

- `should be freed for posture-support expression`

This does not mean "raise ODW."
It means:

- reduce FR's preemptive hold on the same expressive territory

### 2.6 Suspected conversion / continuation pressure that should be more conditional elsewhere

Current reading:

- some geometry continuation pressure currently appears bundled into FR-led persistence
- but semantically, some of that continued forward commitment looks more like something that should depend on later conditional mechanisms such as PD and MB

Why this is suspicious:

- FR should not be the general owner of "keep pushing this shape forward"
- that kind of continuation pressure should be more conditional, more phase-sensitive, and more attributable to later mechanisms

Current governance label:

- `should be left to later conditional mechanisms`

## 3. Authority Reallocation Judgment

### 3.1 Authority that should remain in FR

- direct resistance to deformation
- bounded regroup / restoration authority
- bounded persistence of already-formed structure

These are core FR authorities and should not be erased.

### 3.2 Authority that should be reduced in FR

- early emergence-threshold control
- phase-unspecific always-on reinforcement of protrusion survival
- any gate-like role in deciding whether front geometry enters wedge-capable regime

These are the most suspicious overreach items.

### 3.3 Authority that should be re-expressed through movement substrate

- neutral posture expression space before strong commitment
- front geometry evolution that should remain open to multiple posture outcomes before contact
- substrate-level geometry readiness that should not require high FR to exist

This is the main "substrate purification" direction.

### 3.4 Authority that should be freed for posture-support expression

- readable ODW differentiation in front-shape trend and quality
- posture-family expression that currently looks too conditional on FR already opening the channel

This does not mean ODW becomes a new main gate by decree.
It means the system should stop forcing ODW to speak through FR-preconditioned geometry.

### 3.5 Authority that should be left to later conditional mechanisms

- transient deformation amplification linked to MB
- forward exploitation / continuation pressure linked to PD
- local conversion behavior that should depend on conditional battle state, not just prior FR-led persistence

In short:

```text
FR should hold stabilization.
Movement substrate should hold neutral geometry expressivity.
ODW should regain cleaner posture-support expression.
MB / PD should retain later, conditional deformation and conversion influence.
```

## 4. FR5 Threshold Correction Candidate

### 4.1 Why FR5 remains the main diagnostic window

`FR5` remains the best root-cause diagnostic band because:

- `FR2` is too closed to show nuanced authority distribution
- `FR8` is already close to saturation
- `FR5` sits where later mechanisms can either become readable or remain trapped behind FR preemption

So the purpose of `FR5` correction is now:

```text
not just to weaken FR in the middle band,
but to test whether authority space is actually freed for later mechanisms.
```

### 4.2 Current minimal candidate

Current strongest bounded candidate remains:

- `exp_precontact_plus_fr_centroid_midband_pdhigh_precontact_strong_probe`

Current rule:

```text
if engaged_fraction <= 0.0
   and 0.125 < kappa <= 0.5
   and pd_norm > 0.5:
       kappa_eff = 0.125
else:
       kappa_eff = kappa
```

with:

```text
kappa = (FR_raw - 1) / 8
pd_norm = (PD_raw - 1) / 8
```

Interpretation:

- strict pre-contact only
- FR midband only
- PD-high subset only
- subtractive reduction on the suspect centroid-restoration authority

### 4.3 Why this candidate is still the best current root-cause probe

It does more than merely lower FR.

On the tested `FR5/PD8` subset it:

- removes wedge overreach in the problematic `ODW8` cases
- preserves the tested legitimate `FR5/PD2/ODW8` winner
- preserves `FR2` guardrails
- preserves the tested healthy `FR8` support control

Most importantly, it makes the downstream story more legible:

- `PD8` cases change
- `PD2` cases stay put
- `ODW8` no longer looks like it is expressing through an FR-carried wedge gate in the same way

That is why this candidate is currently better understood as:

```text
authority-space release probe
```

not just:

```text
FR weakening probe
```

### 4.4 Current limitation

The candidate is still narrow.

Current evidence supports:

- release of authority space on the `FR5/PD8` subset

Current evidence does not yet prove:

- that the whole `FR5` band has been cleaned
- that all relevant ODW expression is now substrate-clean
- that `PD5` shoulder cases have become fully interpretable

So the candidate is promising, but still diagnostic rather than integrative.

## 5. Paired Validation Plan

The next bounded validation should not ask only:

```text
Did FR get weaker?
```

It must ask:

```text
Did later mechanisms become more readable on a cleaner substrate?
```

### 5.1 Validation focus

Priority batch should stay centered on `FR5` and nearby controls:

- `FR5` target cells across `PD={5,8}` and `ODW={2,8}`
- selected `MB={2,5,8}` spread
- existing `FR2` guardrails
- existing healthy `FR8` support control

### 5.2 Mandatory readout

Existing readout should be kept:

- `WedgePresent`
- `front_profile`
- `StructuralFragility`
- `c_conn`
- `collapse`
- `FireEff`
- alive trajectory
- divergence timing

Additional interpretation requirement for this stage:

- does `ODW` become more readable as posture-support expression
- do `MB` and `PD` conditional effects become more separable
- is there less evidence that FR must "open the door" before others can speak

### 5.3 Specific acceptance questions

1. Does the candidate reduce `FR5` wedge-gate overreach?
2. Does it preserve legitimate FR stabilization?
3. Does `ODW` read more like support / posture differentiation rather than FR-carried wedge permission?
4. Does `PD` read more like conditional continuation pressure rather than an FR-enabled add-on?
5. Does `MB` remain a transient deformation amplifier rather than a hidden substitute gate?
6. Does the system avoid collapsing into `FR2`-like broad suppression?

## 6. Current Engineering Judgment

The current thread has moved past simple "FR too high" language.

Engineering judgment now is:

```text
The main issue is not only FR over-strength.
It is that FR appears to have historically preempted geometry authority
that should be more cleanly shared across substrate, posture-support, and later conditional mechanisms.
```

Therefore the preferred correction logic is:

- reduce FR preemption
- purify the geometry substrate
- free expression space
- keep FR as stabilizer, not gate-owner

The current `FR5` candidate is useful precisely because it begins to test that reallocation logic without baseline replacement.

## 7. Recommended Next Step

Engineering recommends one bounded next step only:

- continue `FR5`-centered validation under the reallocation lens
- do not reopen high-`FR` cap family as the main line
- do not treat "ODW readability" as "raise ODW"
- do not broaden into new phenomenon search

If governance accepts this framing, subsequent validation should be judged by:

```text
less FR preemption,
cleaner substrate,
clearer later-mechanism expression
```

rather than by wedge suppression alone.
